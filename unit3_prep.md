## Key Concepts

### What are Parts of Speech?
Words are divided into different kinds or classes, called Parts of Speech, according to their use, that is according to the work they do in a sentence. Each part performs a unique function.

The eight parts of speech:
1. Noun
2. Pronoun
3. Verb
4. Adverb
5. Adjective
6. Prepositions
7. Conjunctions
8. Interjections

### Nouns
A noun is a word used as the name of a person, place or thing.

**Types:**
- Common Noun: Shared by all (city, pen)
- Proper Noun: Specific names (India, Sarah) - Capitalized
- Collective Noun: Group (shoal of fish, herd of cattle)
- Abstract Noun: Quality/State (honesty, childhood)

**Classification by Number:**
- Countable: Can be counted (book, pen)
- Uncountable: Cannot be counted (oil, honesty)

### Pronouns
A pronoun is a word used instead of a noun.

**Types:**
- Personal: I, me, we, us, he, him, she, her, it, they, them
- Possessive: My, mine, our, ours, his, her, hers, its, their, theirs
- Relative: Who, that, which
- Reflexive: Myself, himself, herself, ourselves, themselves
- Intensive: Emphasize (I myself)
- Indefinite: Some, anyone, everything
- Demonstrative: This, that, these, those
- Interrogative: Who, whom, whose, which, what
- Reciprocal: Each other, one another
- Distributive: Each, neither, either

### Verbs
A verb expresses action or state.

**Types:**
- Finite: Have tense/person/number (Alex went)
- Non-finite: Infinitive, gerund, participle (going, to go)
- Action: Express action (write, run)
- Linking: Connect subject to complement (is, seem)
- Auxiliary: Help main verb (be, have, do)
- Modal: Express possibility (can, may, must)

### Adverbs
An adverb modifies or qualifies an adjective, verb, or other adverb.

**Types:**
- Manner: How (quickly, beautifully)
- Place: Where (here, everywhere)
- Time: When (early, never)
- Degree: Extent (almost, very)

### Adjectives
An adjective describes a noun or pronoun.

**Degrees:**
- Positive: Normal (good)
- Comparative: Compare two (better)
- Superlative: Highest (best)

**Types:**
- Descriptive: Describe qualities (big, small)
- Quantitative: Quantity (one, many)
- Demonstrative: Point out (this, that)
- Possessive: Ownership (my, his)
- Interrogative: Questions (which, what)
- Distributive: Individual (each, every)

### Prepositions
A preposition shows relationship between noun/pronoun and other words.

**Types:**
- Time: On, at, in, from, to, for, since, ago, before, till/until, by
- Place: On, at, in, by, from, to, towards, up, down, across, between, among, through, in front of, behind, above, over, under, below
- Agents/Things: Of, for, by, with, about

### Conjunctions
A conjunction connects words, phrases, or clauses.

**Types:**
- Coordinating: For, and, nor, but, or, yet, so
- Correlative: Either/or, neither/nor, not only/but also
- Subordinating: After, although, as, as if, as long as, as much as, as soon as, before, even though, even if, only, since, unless, until, till, when, whenever, whereas, while, etc.

### Interjections
An interjection is a word or phrase expressing emotion.

**Examples:**
- Joy: Hurray, Wow, Yippee
- Surprise: What, Eh, Ah
- Grief: Alas, Ouch
- Approval: Bravo, Well done

**Rules:**
- Express sudden mood/emotion
- Interrupt conversation
- Express yes/no
- Get attention

## Key Terms
- **Noun Phrase:** Group of words with noun as head
- **Verb Phrase:** Group including main verb
- **Adjective Clause:** Describes noun
- **Adverb Clause:** Modifies verb/adjective/adverb

## Self Assessment Questions
1. I go to college daily. The underlined word is a _______. (C. common noun)
2. Which of the following is not an abstract noun? (B. beautiful)
3. The fishermen caught a shoal of fish. What is ‘shoal’ in this sentence? (B. collective noun)
4. I am eagerly waiting to meet Sarah. _____ is arriving tomorrow. (C. She)
5. Could you please pass me the bowl? The underlined word is a _______. (C. pronoun)
6. The girl walked briskly. The underlined word is a/an _______. (A. adverb)
7. The student solved the sums himself. The underlined word is a _______ pronoun. (B. reflexive)
8. I went _____ the market. (C. to)
9. My friend _____ I went to a concert. (D. and)
10. I will see you ____ Saturday. (A. on)
11. We drove ______the tunnel. (D. through)
12. ________swimming, I also like dancing. (D. Besides)
13. A word or a phrase that is used as a short, sudden expression of emotion is called ______. (D. an interjection)
14. I will sit in the library. The underlined word is ________ (C. a noun)
15. Oh my God! You look gorgeous. The underlined phrase is ______. (B. an interjection)
