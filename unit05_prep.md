# Unit 5 Exam Preparation: Listening Skills

## Objectives
After studying this unit, you will be able to:
- Acknowledge that listening is not a passive activity
- Understand that listening skills significantly contribute to effective communication skills
- Establish key differences between hearing and listening

## Key Concepts

### What are Listening Skills?
Listening is the ability to accurately receive and interpret messages in the communication process. It’s how we make sense of, assess, and respond to what we hear.

### Differences between Hearing and Listening
- **Hearing:** Ability to perceive sounds (physiological, subconscious, passive).
- **Listening:** Conscious interpretation and analysis of sounds (psychological, active, requires concentration).

### Characteristics of Listening
- Accurate reception and interpretation
- Key to effective communication
- Soft skill for employers
- Involves observing body language and non-verbal cues

### Plight of Listening
Modern life discourages listening due to noise, digital distractions, etc. Listening is more valuable than speaking.

### Principles of Listening
- Listen to what is said and unsaid
- Observe body language
- Stop talking
- Prepare yourself
- Focus on speaker
- Put distractions aside
- Put speaker at ease
- Remove distractions
- Empathize
- Be patient
- Avoid prejudice
- Listen to tone
- Listen for ideas, not words
- Watch for non-verbal cues

### HURIER Model of Listening
- **H - Hearing:** Physical and non-verbal signals
- **U - Understanding:** Tie elements together
- **R - Remembering:** Focus to remember message
- **I - Interpreting:** Consider context, avoid biases
- **E - Evaluating:** Open mind, don't jump to conclusions
- **R - Responding:** Well-measured response

### Active Listening
- Pay attention
- Show you're listening (nod, facial expressions)
- Provide feedback (paraphrase)
- Defer judgment
- Respond appropriately

### Types of Listening
- **Appreciative:** For enjoyment (music, sermons)
- **Empathetic:** Show concern, understand perspective
- **Comprehensive:** Understand message (news, lectures)
- **Critical:** Evaluate content (buying decisions, medical advice)

### How to Become a Good Listener
- Put phone away
- Learn to have discussions without arguing
- Let thoughts flow out to return to listening
- Practice mindful listening
- Talk less, listen more
- Put yourself in others' shoes

### Importance of Listening
- Key to communication
- Builds relationships
- Shows maturity and respect
- Personal development
- Resolves issues
- Career enhancement

## Key Terms
- **Active Listening:** Fully concentrating on speaker
- **Empathetic Listening:** Understanding from speaker's perspective
- **Non-verbal Cues:** Body language, tone
- **HURIER Model:** Framework for effective listening

## Self Assessment Questions
1. A study in the UK showed that the mere presence of a ________ made people in a chat more likely to see the other as unfriendly. (B. phone)
2. Good listening skills do not lead to ____________. (A. arguments)
3. Critical listening is listening to ________ the content of the message. (A. evaluate)
4. How many principles of listening are there? (D. 14)
5. A good listener will also listen to _______ messages besides verbal messages. (C. non-verbal)
6. Listening is not just a matter of using your ears, but also your _______. (B. eyes)
7. Hearing is ___________. (C. an ability)
8. Listening is ___________. (A. a skill)
9. In the words of Stephen R. Covey, most people do not listen with the intent to understand, they listen with the __________. (B. intent to reply)
10. Which of the following is not a principle of listening? (D. Intelligence)
11. In order to become an active listener, one needs to defer ________. (A. judgment)
12. How many types of listening are there? (B. 4)
13. If you try to listen by putting yourself in the speaker’s shoes, then you are __________. (D. an empathetic listener)
14. ___________ is an active mental process. (C. Listening)
15. Does active listening indicate moving while listening? (A. No)
