## Key Concepts

### Common Errors and Corrections

1. **There/Their/They're**
   - There: Place (I would like to visit there.)
   - Their: Possession (Their project.)
   - They're: They are (They're invited.)

2. **Its/It's**
   - Its: Possession (Its color is black.)
   - It's: It is (It's our responsibility.)

3. **Fewer/Less**
   - Fewer: Countable nouns (Fewer cakes.)
   - Less: Uncountable nouns (Less sugar.)

4. **Than/Then**
   - Than: Comparison (Better than him.)
   - Then: Time/Sequence (First, then.)

5. **Amount/Number**
   - Amount: Uncountable (Amount of sugar.)
   - Number: Countable (Number of books.)

6. **Too/To/Two**
   - Too: Very/Also (Too naïve; me too.)
   - To: Preposition/Infinitive (Listen to you; want to eat.)
   - Two: Number (Two books.)

7. **Apostrophes**
   - Plural: No apostrophe (pens for sale)
   - Possession: Apostrophe (Jane’s horse)
   - Contraction: Apostrophe (1980s, it’s)

8. **Should of/Could of/Would of**
   - Incorrect: Should of gone.
   - Correct: Should’ve gone (Should have).

9. **Me and John/John and I**
   - Incorrect: Me and John are going.
   - Correct: John and I are going.

10. **Invite/Invitation**
    - Invite: Verb (Invite her.)
    - Invitation: Noun (Send an invitation.)

11. **Who/Whom**
    - Who: Subject (Who is responsible?)
    - Whom: Object (Whom shall I invite?)

12. **Affect/Effect**
    - Affect: Verb (Affect the environment.)
    - Effect: Noun (Negative effect.)

13. **i.e./e.g.**
    - i.e.: That is (id est)
    - e.g.: For example (exempli gratia)

14. **Since/For**
    - Since: Point in time (Since morning.)
    - For: Period of time (For two days.)

15. **Subject-Verb Agreement**
    - Every student likes the teacher. (Not students)

16. **Redundant Conjunctions**
    - Although it was raining, we had the picnic. (Not but)

17. **Other Errors**
    - Although it was raining, but we had the picnic. → Although it was raining, we had the picnic.
    - I enjoyed from the movie. → I enjoyed the movie.
    - I look forward to meet you. → I look forward to meeting you.
    - I like very much ice cream. → I like ice cream very much.
    - My husband is engineer. → My husband is an engineer.
    - I came Australia to study English. → I came to Australia to study English.
    - It is more hot now. → It is hotter now.
    - You can give me an information? → Can you give me the information?
    - They cooked the dinner themself. → They cooked the dinner themselves.
    - The life is hard! → Life is hard!
    - How many childrens you have? → How many children do you have?
    - My brother has 10 years. → My brother is 10 years old.
    - I want eat now. → I want to eat now.
    - You are very nice, as your mother. → You are very nice like your mother.
    - She said me that she liked you. → She told me that she liked you.

## Key Terms
- **Homophones:** Words that sound the same but differ in meaning (there/their/they're)
- **Contraction:** Shortened form (it's = it is)
- **Possessive:** Showing ownership (Jane’s)
- **Redundancy:** Unnecessary repetition

## Self Assessment Questions
1. ‘The sceneries of Meghalaya are changing’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is _________ (B. The scenery of Meghalaya is changing.)
2. ‘Good girls do not move in bad companies’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ________ (A. Good girls do not move in bad company.)
3. ‘I shall see them in there house soon’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is _______ (C. I shall see them in their house soon.)
4. ‘He did not brought any gift for you’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is _________ (D. He did not bring any gift for you.)
5. ‘The boy does not sings a song’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ___________ (A. The boy does not sing a song.)
6. ‘I have not met her from 2020’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ___________ (D. I have not met her since 2020.)
7. ‘She shall revert back’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ___________ (B. She shall revert.)
8. ‘It has been raining from afternoon’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ____________ (C. It has been raining since afternoon.)
9. ‘Me and my friends likes to visit the beach’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ____________ (A. My friends and I like to visit the beach.)
10. ‘He belongs to United States of America’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ____________ (B. He belongs to the United States of America.)
11. ‘Children is playing in the ground’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ____________ (C. Children are playing in the ground.)
12. ‘Her opinion does not effect me’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is _______________ (D. Her opinion does not affect me.)
13. ‘She is my cousin sister’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ___________ (A. She is my cousin.)
14. ‘She eats the orange daily’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is __________ (C. She eats an orange daily.)
15. ‘He jumped in the river’ is an incorrect sentence. The correct sentence is ___________ (D. He jumped into the river.)
