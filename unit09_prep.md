# Unit 9 Exam Preparation: Speaking Skills - Fluency, Intelligibility, Non-verbal, Business Communication

## Objectives
After studying this unit, you will be able to:
- Understand the meaning and importance of fluency, intelligibility, non-verbal communication and business communication in English
- Become well versed with the problems faced in every aspect of speaking skills
- Get an overview of the ways by which you can improve all the essential speaking skills

## Key Concepts

### Fluency
Ability to speak easily, well, and quickly.

**Problems:**
- Lack of vocabulary
- Poor grammar knowledge
- Pronunciation issues
- Weak listening ability
- Anxiety

**Ways to Improve:**
- Think in English
- Pay attention to stressed sounds
- Talk to yourself
- Master English registers
- Use a mirror
- Practice daily
- Read regularly
- Give structure to writing (but for speaking: practice speaking)

### Intelligibility
How clearly speech is comprehensible.

**Factors Affecting:**
- L1 background
- Proficiency

**Ways to Improve:**
- Record your speech
- Listening and shadowing
- Target problem sounds
- Transcribe and drill vocabulary
- Focus on sentence stress, rhythm, intonation
- Say all speech sounds, especially at ends
- Learn prosody rules
- Smooth out hesitations
- Increase volume
- Look after voice
- Slow down
- Pretend speaking into microphone
- Don’t multitask

### Non-Verbal Communication
Communication without words: facial expressions, body language, tone, space, time.

**Types:**
- **Kinesics:** Body movements, gestures, eye contact, facial expressions
- **Haptics:** Touch
- **Vocalics:** Volume, rate, pitch, vocal quality
- **Proxemics:** Personal space
- **Chronemics:** Time

### Business Communication
Process of sharing information in business settings.

**Types:**
- **Internal:** Upward, downward, lateral, diagonal
- **External:** With customers, vendors, etc.

## Key Terms
- **Fluency:** Smooth, effortless speech
- **Intelligibility:** Clarity of speech
- **Non-verbal Cues:** Body language, etc.
- **Business Communication:** Professional information sharing

## Self Assessment Questions
1. According to Beardsmore, oral fluency needs __________ competence to formulate more than one sentence. (A. communicative)
2. Day and Shapson give more explanations of fluency at ________ levels. (B. four)
3. ___________ mentions that pronunciation is the phonological process which enables students to generate clearer spoken language. (C. Gerard)
4. Which of the following is not a problem in achieving fluency in English? (D. Sartorial sense)
5. A __________ is a different set of words and phrases that you use depending on the situation you find yourself in. (A. register)
6. According to Smith and Nelson, __________ is defined as the listener’s ability to understand the meaning of utterances. (A. comprehensibility)
7. Cutler argues that listeners first perceive the ________ of the speech they hear rather than ________ and grammar. (B. phonology, lexicon)
8. What is intelligibility according to Jenkins? (C. Phonological intelligibility)
9. Which of the following does not affect intelligibility? (D. Sartorial sense)
10. __________ involves trying to say the same words as the speaker at about the same time or as fast as possible. (A. Shadowing)
11. In the words of Rob Brown, ‘If you can speak, you can ________. If you can influence, you can _______ lives.’ (B. influence, change)
12. Any communication that comes from a subordinate to a manager is referred to as_________ business communication. (A. upward)
13. Group meetings are an example of________ business communication. (C. lateral)
14. Non-verbal communication does not include_______. (A. sartorial sense)
15. ‘Emblems’ fall under the category of_______. (C. gestures)
