# Unit 6 Exam Preparation: Listening Skills - Types of Listening

## Objectives
After studying this unit, you will be able to:
- Understand the meaning and importance of informational listening, critical and empathetic listening
- Discuss the differences between various types of listening
- Become a good listener
- Improve your listening behavior to enhance communication skills

## Key Concepts

### Informational Listening
Primary concern is to understand the message. Success when meaning assigned matches sender's intent.

**Variables:**
- **Vocabulary:** Increase vocabulary to improve understanding.
- **Concentration:** Requires discipline; avoid distractions.
- **Memory:** Helps recall experiences, establish expectations, understand others.

**Barriers:**
- **Confirmation Bias:** Tendency to pick aspects supporting own beliefs.
- **Vividness Effect:** Graphic events affect perception disproportionately.

**Improving Informational Listening:**
- Paraphrase what was said
- Watch for confirmation bias
- Realize beliefs may not be accurate
- Listen more for substance than style
- Ask questions to yourself

### Critical Listening
Listening to evaluate the content of the message. Make mental judgments based on facts.

**Improving Critical Listening:**
- Recognize facts vs. opinions
- Uncover assumptions
- Be open to new ideas
- Rely on reason and common sense
- Take notes
- Listen ethically

### Empathetic Listening
Structured technique to develop relationships through understanding intellectually and emotionally.

**Techniques:**
- Develop good listening habits (don't interrupt, show interest, etc.)
- Listen to understand (put aside own needs)
- Practice four stages: Copy, Say what heard, Reflect feeling, Restate
- Avoid misunderstandings
- Follow rules: Pay attention to feelings, don't put own feelings first, communicate responsively, etc.

**Qualities of Empathetic Listener:**
- Presence: Focused on conversation
- Compassion: Identify with speaker's experience
- Wisdom: Understand circumstances
- Non-judgment: Avoid criticizing

## Key Terms
- **Informational Listening:** Listening to understand
- **Critical Listening:** Listening to evaluate
- **Empathetic Listening:** Listening with concern and understanding
- **Confirmation Bias:** Favoring information that confirms beliefs
- **Vividness Effect:** Overemphasis on dramatic events

## Self Assessment Questions
1. In the words of Stephen Covey, “In empathetic listening, you listen with your ears, but you also, more importantly, listen with your ______ and your ______.” (B. eyes, heart)
2. Empathetic listening provides a higher level of understanding of how others _______. (A. feel)
3. Which of the following is not a technique used for developing empathetic listening? (C. Flout the rules of empathetic listening)
4. Which of the following is the Stage 1 of empathetic listening? (B. Copy what is being said)
5. Which of the following is not a quality of an empathetic listener? (D. Pride)
6. Critical listening is about _______ the content of the message. (B. evaluating)
7. If someone is listening critically, then he/she is also ________ critically. (A. thinking)
8. Assumptions are not supported by _______. (C. evidence)
9. Which of the following is not a way to improve critical listening? (D. Optimism)
10. In the words of Jeanette Winterson, ‘Everything in writing begins with ________ which begins with _______. (A. language, listening)
11. One can become a better informational listener by listening more for __________ than style. (A. substance)
12. _________ and _________ are the two barriers to informational listening. (B. Confirmation bias, vividness effect)
13. Confirmation bias is the tendency to pick out aspects of a conversation that support one‘s _______ and _______. (C. beliefs, values)
14. Confirmation bias detracts from a person‘s ability to be ___________. (D. open minded)
15. Which of the following is not an example of informational listening? (C. Listening to a friend who is upset)
