# Unit 13 Exam Preparation: Writing Skills - Paragraph Writing

## Objectives
After studying this unit, you will be able to:
- Learn different ways of arranging sentences to construct a meaningful paragraph
- Revise the rules of rearrangement of jumbled sentences
- Become well versed with different aspects of constructing a paragraph
- Understand the rules of writing a paragraph in English
- Employ effective tools for writing a well organized paragraph

## Key Concepts

### Strategies for Sentence Rearrangement
- Identify topic sentence (often first or last, contains main idea).
- Look for pronouns (he, she, it refer back).
- Articles: 'A' first, then 'An', 'The'.
- Conjunctions: 'But' before 'So' before 'Now'.
- Words like 'Hence', 'Therefore' last.
- Simple to compound to complex sentences.

### Components of a Good Paragraph
- **Topic Sentence:** Introduces main idea.
- **Supporting Sentences:** Provide details, examples.
- **Concluding Sentence:** Summarizes or transitions.

### Paragraph Writing
- **Structure:** Topic sentence, body, conclusion.
- **Organizing:**
  - **Narration:** Tell story chronologically.
  - **Description:** Specific details spatially.
  - **Process:** Step-by-step.
  - **Classification:** Group parts.
  - **Illustration:** Examples supporting point.

**Characteristics:**
- Unified
- Clearly related to thesis
- Coherent
- Well-developed

### Common Mistakes
- **Stylistic/Technical:** Wrong italics, titles, punctuation.
- **Structural Continuity:** Mismatched thesis order.
- **Language:** Inconsistent tense, contractions, slang, first/second person, self-references.
- **Transition:** Missing linkers.
- **Ambiguous Reference:** Unclear pronouns.
- **Analysis:** Missing PEE (Point, Evidence, Explanation).

## Key Terms
- **Topic Sentence:** Main idea
- **Supporting Details:** Evidence
- **Concluding Sentence:** Wrap-up
- **Narration:** Storytelling
- **Description:** Detailing

## Self Assessment Questions
1. _________and________ are required to rearrange the sentences into a meaningful sentence. (A. Cohesion, coherence)
2. The sentences should be arranged in such a way that the paragraph is in accordance with the syntaxes and_________. (B. semantics)
3. If a sentence begins with word ‘initially’, it is most likely to be placed at the_______ of a paragraph. (C. start)
4. If a sentence begins with pronouns like ‘you’ or ‘I’, it is not likely to be placed in the_________ of a paragraph. (D. beginning)
5. If a sentence begins with ‘a’, ‘an’ or ‘the’, can it come at the start of a paragraph? (A. Yes)
6. Which of the following is not required to construct a good paragraph? (A. Body language)
7. Phrases like ‘in addition’ are referred to as_________ words. (B. transition)
8. A working_______ functions like a seed from which your paper and your ideas will grow. (C. thesis)
9. What is called as the ‘germination process ’in paragraph writing? (D. Conception of ideas)
10. Who among the following have devised rules for the length of a paragraph? (A. Strunk and White)
11. Which of the following is not a rule in paragraph writing? (B. Use of slangs)
12. ‘Sarah would do anything for Susan because she loves her like a sister.’ This above sentence is an example of ____________. (C. Ambiguous pronoun reference)
13. The natural genius of the__________ structure was favorable to paragraph writing. (D. Anglo-Saxon)
14. The paragraph as we know it comes into something like settled shape in___________. (A. Sir William Temple)
15. ‘Hence, I cancelled the meeting.’ Where will you place this sentence in a paragraph? (B. End)
